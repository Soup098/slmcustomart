Follow these tips when photographing your pet.

Great photos make for a great portrait. A few simple things will help me capture your pet’s personality exactly the way you love it.

Tip #1:

Get down to your pet’s eye level. A photo taken at their height feels far more natural and personal than one looking down.

Tip #2:

Use soft, natural light — near a window or outside in the shade. Avoid the flash, which flattens fur and can cause glowing eyes.

Tip #3:

Take a few clear close-ups of the face and eyes, where your pet’s personality really shows — plus at least one full-body shot.

Tip #4:

Include any markings, colors, or features you’d like captured, and send multiple angles so I can choose the most flattering.

Tip #5:

Favorite candid moments are wonderful too. I can remove leashes, backgrounds, or anything you don’t want in the portrait — learn more about your personalization options.
