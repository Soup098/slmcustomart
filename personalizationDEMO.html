It’s the little things that make a big impact.

Every portrait carries a story. Before a single brushstroke, I learn what makes a home or a moment matter to the people who live it — then I weave those details into the painting. Here are a few of those stories.

A Lifetime of Summers “

The Summer Cottage

I painted this portrait for a man who has cherished his summers at this cottage for most of his life. To capture his family’s love for the home, I included the hammock, the colorful bicycle whirligig, and a few ducks. While 10–20 ducks usually visit for corn, I kept them minimal so the message of love stayed clear.

Home, Already Loved “

A Military Family’s Home

I painted this for a military family who had just purchased this lovely home. I included the flag and pole that were already on the property — a detail that mattered deeply to them. The birds in the painting came from the buyers’ request to honor the feathered residents who already called the house home. Those small touches made a big impact.

Flag & flagpole Resident birds Existing landscaping
More Than a House “

The Lake Camp

I painted this for very good friends who have a summer “camp” on the lake. It isn’t a home portrait, but it’s a hugely important part of their lives. The boat on the water is their own — and although the people aboard aren’t recognizable from a distance, they know exactly who they are. It’s all about the little touches.
